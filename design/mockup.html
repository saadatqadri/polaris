# The Shape of a Morning

There is a particular silence before the first sentence — the kind that asks to be broken slowly, by hand.

Polaris begins there. No panels, no palettes, no assistant hovering at your elbow. Just a warm page, one good typeface, and a cursor keeping time. The words that land here are *yours* — typed, reconsidered, typed again. That friction is the point. It is what thinking feels like.

## What stays out of the way

  • - The chrome fades while you type, and returns when you rest
  • - Your words are saved as they land — no dialogs, no anxiety
  • - Markdown stays visible, but **quiet**
> Write with the door closed, rewrite with the door open.

The tools should hold still so the writing can move. When you want the machine’s opinion, you will ask for it — and not a moment before.
